STARTUP KNOWLEDGE BASE — Legal Tech & AI Startups
==================================================

CASE STUDY: Harvey AI
Founded: 2022 | Funding: $100M+ Series B
Focus: AI legal assistant for BigLaw (500+ attorney firms)
Model: GPT-4 powered, trained on legal corpus + firm data
Key customers: A&O Shearman, PwC Legal, Paul Hastings, Allen & Overy
Pricing: Enterprise only (~$5,000-20,000/month per firm)
Strength: Deep BigLaw relationships, trained on premium legal data
Weakness: Inaccessible to small/mid firms. No self-serve. Sales-heavy.

CASE STUDY: Ironclad
Founded: 2014 | Funding: $333M total (Series D $100M in 2021)
Focus: Contract Lifecycle Management (CLM) for enterprise
Key customers: L'Oreal, Mastercard, Dropbox, Monday.com
Pricing: Enterprise custom (~$30K-200K/year)
Strength: Deep workflow integration, strong brand in legal ops
Weakness: Not AI-first, complex implementation (3-6 months)

CASE STUDY: Clio (Benchmark for mid-market legal SaaS)
Founded: 2008 | Funding: $1.1B total | Valuation: $1.6B
Focus: Practice management for small/mid law firms
Customers: 150,000+ law firms worldwide
Pricing: $49-149/user/month
Strength: PLG, massive distribution, brand recognition
Insight: 70% of customers have <10 lawyers — huge addressable market

CASE STUDY: ContractPodAi
Founded: 2012 | Funding: $115M Series C
Focus: Enterprise CLM with AI (AI-powered contract intelligence)
Customers: Major enterprises (Shell, Amazon, Sony)
Weakness: Complex, expensive, requires dedicated implementation team

MARKET INSIGHT: The Mid-Market Gap
=====================================
- 500,000+ law firms globally; 80% have <10 attorneys
- Harvey and Ironclad target the top 0.5% of firms by size
- Clio dominates practice management but lacks AI document analysis
- No AI-native solution exists for the $50M-500M revenue firm segment
- This is the opportunity: AI document analysis, affordable, self-serve

KEY METRICS FOR LEGAL TECH B2B SAAS (Industry Benchmarks):
- Average contract review time saved with AI: 88% (McKinsey 2023)
- Law firm software spend: $18,000/yr average (solo-small firms)
- AI legal tool adoption: 73% of firms plan adoption by 2025
- NRR for legal tech SaaS: 110-125% (high stickiness)
- Sales cycle: 1-3 months for SMB, 6-18 months for enterprise

COMPETITIVE MOAT FRAMEWORK FOR LEGAL AI:
1. Data moat: Every document analyzed trains better models
2. Integration moat: Deep Clio/PracticePanther integration = sticky
3. Community moat: Attorney networks and bar association relationships
4. Compliance moat: First to achieve ABA ethics opinion + SOC2

FUNDRAISING BENCHMARKS (Seed Stage, Legal Tech, 2024):

- Key metrics for seed: 3-10 paying customers, NPS >50
